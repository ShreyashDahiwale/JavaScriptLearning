# What is the difference between Authentication and Authorization?
- *Authentication*:
    - Authentication is about **proving who you are**. 
    - It verifies your identity.
    - Happens before you access anything.
    - It's like showing your ID card and Passport at Airport. Security checks the identity and verify 

- *Authorization*:
    - Authorization is about *what you are allowed to do*.
    - Once the airport security knows who you are, they check if you have the permission to enter certain areas - like lounges, customs, or the boarding gate.
    - After authentication, the system checks what resources, data, or actions you are allowed to do based on your role and privileges.
    - It controls access levels and permissions. 
    - Determine what files, features, or services you can use. 
    - Happens after successful authentication. 

- **Authentication asks**: “Who are you?”
- **Authorization asks**: “What can you do?”
